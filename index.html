Who I Am

About Me

I'm a Data Science and Cognitive Science student at UC Berkeley, graduating May 2027, with a genuine curiosity about how data, computation, and the human mind intersect.

My work spans machine learning, data engineering, and robotics — I enjoy breaking broad, fuzzy problems into testable modules and iterating until something real emerges. Whether it's cleaning noisy sensor data, building a neural net from scratch, or coordinating a robotic arm, I care about the craft.

I grew up between Iran and the U.S., which gave me a dual lens: an appreciation for depth of culture alongside the pragmatism of building things. Music, neuroscience, and human behavior are the threads that run through most of what I build and study.

Currently: student data analyst at the Othering & Belonging Institute, studying abroad in Padua, Italy (Fall 2026), and always working on a side project.
